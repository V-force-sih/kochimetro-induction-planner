The AI Induction Planner Workflow

Our AI system follows a methodical four-stage process to ensure the most efficient and reliable train scheduling, from data collection to continuous learning.

Stage 1 - Data Ingestion

The system collects vast amounts of **real-time data** from sources like train sensors, station footfall, maintenance logs, and live network status to create a comprehensive operational picture.

Stage 2 - Optimization

An **AI engine** processes the ingested data to generate the most efficient train schedule. It considers multiple variables such as energy consumption, fleet availability, and passenger demand to minimize operational costs.

Stage 3 - Scheduling

The final, **optimized plan** is deployed to the operational control system. This stage automates the process of scheduling train movements, adjusting to real-time events and ensuring a smooth, reliable service.

Stage 4 - ML Model Learning from Historical Data

The system continuously **learns** from the implemented schedules and real-world outcomes. By analyzing historical data, the ML model identifies patterns and refines its algorithms to improve the accuracy and efficiency of future plans.
